Body Mass Index (BMI)

A simple tool to estimate healthy body weight

What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a number calculated from a person's weight and height. It's used as a simple method to assess whether a person is underweight, at a healthy weight, overweight, or obese.

  • Formula (Metric): weight (kg) / height (ms2)
  • Formula (Imperial): weight (lb) × 703 / height (in2)

Limitations of BMI:

  • Doesn't differentiate between muscle and fat (so muscular people may appear "overweight")
  • Doesn't consider fat distribution (like visceral fat)
  • Less accurate for elderly people, athletes, and certain ethnic groups

BMI is a screening tool and not a diagnostic of the body fatness or health of an individual.
